City Club, Lucknow · A lifetime begins
Chapter One
The First Hello
It all started on November 3rd with a simple WhatsApp text. By 9:30 PM that night, a call that felt like a reunion with a soul known for lifetimes.

The magic was instant.
Some connections don't build over time — they arrive whole, as if two souls had simply found their way back to where they always belonged.
By November 16th, Prajwal's family made their way to Shahganj, where Shiwangi and her family received them with warmth that felt like it had always been there. He was in Pune, phone in hand, smiling at every update — already feeling like the luckiest person in the room he wasn't even in.
Chapter Two
Written in the Stars
November 25th: Prajwal flew from Pune to Lucknow. Three days later, his father asked: "Are you sure?" The answer was instant: "100%."

At Starbucks, while families prepared the shagun, forty stolen minutes were more than enough. They already knew.
The Roka. The Tika. The Mithai. And a parking lot that proved the universe approved.
On November 29th, Shiwangi arrived in a beautiful Chikan Kurta — and Hazratganj had never felt so cinematic. At The Hazelnut Factory, over their first official date, they talked the way only two people who have known each other forever can — unhurried, unguarded, completely at home in each other's words.

Then came Platform 1 of Lucknow Railway Station. Hand in hand on a cold November night, they sat and laughed and talked some more — as if the whole world had quietened down just for them. When the train finally pulled in, they settled into Coach A3, Berth 29. A sleepless night — not because sleep wouldn't come, but because neither of them wanted it to. Every passing station felt like a page turning in a story they never wanted to end.

Delhi, the morning after. A metro ride to Millennium City Centre, Gurugram — then they parted briefly, she to her PG, he to his Bhaiya and Bhabhi's home. But only briefly. Hours later, reunited, they drove to Aerocity near New Delhi's international airport — where cake was cut for Bhaiya and Bhabhi's anniversary, a little early, because Prajwal had a flight to catch and some celebrations simply cannot wait.

His brother pulled him aside — "Go, take a walk, just the two of you." They did. Photographs were clicked. Moments were made quietly, the way the best ones always are. Then Bhaiya, Bhabhi, their little 1.5-year-old Raghav, and Shiwangi all drove him to Terminal 1.
She walked him to Gate 1 of Departures. No grand words — just the kind of goodbye that makes you certain you are leaving behind someone worth coming back to.
And then he was back in Pune. But nothing was quite the same anymore.
